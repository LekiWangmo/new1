Staff Task

1. Front Office

-To oversee front desk operations and manage other front-office employees. This can involve managing schedules, delegating specific tasks to different members of the team and taking overall responsibility for the customer experience. Training, coaching and hiring activities are also common.


2. Guest Service

-Hotels may have a separate guest service manager, who takes responsibility for the various guest services provided by the front office. In addition to overseeing core tasks, a guest service manager is likely to be responsible for hiring, training, and otherwise managing reception staff, concierge staff, and others in similar roles.


3. Reservation

-A reservations manager takes control of activities and operations related to room reservations. This includes managing bookings, cancellations and special requests from customers. It also requires coordination with the sales and marketing department, and an ability to deal with guests who arrive and want a room without pre-booking.


4. Revenue

-Within the front office hotel department, a revenue manager is tasked with optimising revenue generation and overall financial performance.


5. Night Audit

-Night auditing involves a variety of tasks, like ensuring that all administrative work has been handled over the course of the day, cash handling, and looking over billing information, in order to identify any potential issues. A night audit manager oversees all of this and helps to keep the hotel running smoothly.


6. Housekeeping

-Takes control of all activities related to cleaning rooms and providing a safe, clean and pleasant environment for guests. They coordinate housekeeping staff, ensure all rooms are turned over on time and are also in charge of cleaning public areas of the hotel, such as restrooms and hallways.
